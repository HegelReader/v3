Y-Acquisitions




Entrepreneurship via acquisition provides students with an existing company as a head start for their ideas and creativity.

* 10,000 Boomers are retiring daily.
* Baby Boomers are expected to transfer $30 trillion in wealth to younger generations over the next many years.
* 19% own small businesses and most do not have a succession plan.
* Most of Millennials & Gen Z would prefer to be TikTok famous than own a plumbing company even if it is doing millions in profit

Problem Solved

Starting is a struggle: Entrepreneurial students struggle to start from scratch.
Inexperience: Students lack the experience starting, running, and scaling real companies.
Lack of funds: Students struggle to get the funds to convert ideas into reality.
Fall-Through: Even students that do start companies quit, sell early, or leave the company to die.

We strive to provide entrepreneurial students the resources to buy and scale already existing companies.







Search & Acquire

Entrepreneurs partner with the fund and its acquisition support team to find a company to acquire with a planned exit in mind.

Scale

Entrepreneurs scale company recruiting BYU students to fill various roles.

Successful BYU alumni will be willing to advise entrepreneurs on specific obstacles to growth. Students of all majors, such as advertising, engineering, IS, etc., can participate in the various roles a company needs. The real-world experience looks great for resumes and interviews. In some cases, their roles could turn into full-time roles after graduation.

Exit/Hold

Company is sold back to entrepreneurs via seller financing or becomes a portfolio company.

If the company is held as a portfolio company of the fund, this will allow entrepreneurs to fill C-Suite roles, giving them the experience of running tangible, working businesses
