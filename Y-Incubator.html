Y-Incubator Fund

A Student Driven Search Fund



Starting is a struggle: Entrepreneurial students struggle to start from scratch. | Inexperience: Students lack the experience starting, running, and scaling real companies.

Lack of funds: Students struggle to get the funds to convert ideas into reality. | Fall-Through: Even students that do start companies quit, sell early, or leave the company to die.


Mission Statement: We strive to provide an entrepreneurial search fund where students buy and scale already existing companies.


How it works:
  1. Search
    • Entrepreneurs partner with the fund to find a company to acquire.
    • Students interested in pursuing careers on the buy-side or sell-side can gain experience as
      a private equity intern assisting the searchers in sourcing and performing due diligence on
      various industries and companies.
  2. Acquire
    • The fund purchases the company with planned exit.
    • Companies start via Sandbox often sell early or are abandoned. The funds serves as an
      intra-university marketplace for good ideas where students can confidently and successfully
      their companies. This keeps successful companies and talent within BYU.
  3. Scale
    • Entrepreneurs scale company recruiting BYU students to fill various roles.
    • Successful BYU alumni will be willing to advise entrepreneurs on specific obstacles to growth
    • Students of all majors, such as advertising, engineering, IS, etc., can participate in the various
      roles a company needs. The real-world experience looks great for resumes and interviews. In
      some cases, their roles could turn into full-time roles after graduation.
  4. Exit/Hold
    • Company is sold back to entrepreneurs via seller financing or becomes a portfolio company.
    • If the company is held as a portfolio company of the fund, this will allow entrepreneurs to
      fill C-Suite roles, giving them the experience of running tangible, working businesses
  5. Reinvest
    • All returns are recycled back into the fund to further our purposes.

Donor-Advised Fund Model:
  1. Immediate Tax Benefits
    • The fund can rely on donors to BYU to raise capital. All money donated will receive
      charity status from the government and can be immediately written off.
  2. Donor Benefits
    • Although donors cannot expect to receive a return on their donation, other strategic
      ways of incentivizing donors include possible awarding debt or equity opportunities
      and a new network of future.
  3. 2% Management Fee
    • The fund will charge a 2% management fee to pay for fund management and student
      internship employment. This helps to retain high quality talent.
